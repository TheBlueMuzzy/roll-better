**Player row layout for landscape:**

In portrait, player rows stacked with 0.9 Z spacing below goal row. In landscape, less Z room available. The space between goal row (Z=-3.2) and rolling zone (Z=-0.5) is 2.7 units. Need to fit human player row + up to 7 AI rows.

New row spacing: 0.55 units apart (vs 0.9 in portrait).
- Human player row: Z = -2.4
- AI row 0: Z = -1.85
- AI row 1: Z = -1.30
- AI row 2: Z = -0.75
- (Additional AI rows would overlap rolling zone — but max visible AI rows in practice is 3-4 before it gets too crowded. The game supports up to 7 other players but they compress naturally.)

Row spacing of 0.55 with DIE_SIZE ≈ 0.8 means rows are tighter than the die height. This is fine — locked dice are rendered at DIE_SIZE * 0.9 visual size and rows don't physically collide (they're display elements, not physics bodies).
