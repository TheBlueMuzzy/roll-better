<task type="auto">
  <name>Task 1: Audio-visual sync audit + timing fixes</name>
  <files>src/utils/soundManager.ts, src/components/AnimatingDie.tsx, src/components/MitosisDie.tsx, src/components/HUD.tsx</files>
  <action>
Review all sound trigger points and verify timing alignment:

1. **Dice impact sounds:** Should fire AT collision moment (onContactForce is synchronous — should be fine). Verify no perceivable delay.

2. **Lock snap timing:** AnimatingDie onComplete fires at lerp end — snap should feel like the die "clicking" into the slot. If it feels late, fire slightly before 100% (at ~95% progress).

3. **Mitosis phase boundaries:** Verify rumble starts exactly when shake begins (0.5s), pop fires exactly when split starts (1.3s). Adjust if sounds feel disconnected from visuals.

4. **Score tick rate:** If ticks are too rapid for small scores (1-2 points) or too slow for large scores (6-8 points), adjust the throttle. Target: each point gets a distinct tick regardless of score size.

5. **Overall volume balance pass:**
   - Play a full game and note any sounds that are too loud or too quiet relative to others
   - Adjust individual sound volumes in SoundManager
   - Target hierarchy: dice impacts (loudest) > lock snaps > mitosis > score ticks > UI clicks (quietest)

6. **Mute when volume = 0:** Verify that audioVolume = 0 in settings produces complete silence. If AudioContext is still processing, add an early return in each play function when volume is 0.

Fix any timing or volume issues found. This is a tuning task — small adjustments, not new features.
  </action>
  <verify>Play a full game (2-3 rounds minimum). All sounds feel synchronized with their visual events. No sound feels early, late, or out of place. Volume at 0 = complete silence. Volume at 100 = comfortable maximum.</verify>
  <done>Audio-visual sync confirmed. Volume hierarchy balanced. Zero-volume produces silence. No timing mismatches.</done>
</task>
